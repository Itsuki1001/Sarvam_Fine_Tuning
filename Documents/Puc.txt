# PUC Certificate: How to Get, Download, and Check Status Online

With digital services becoming widely available, many vehicle-related services can now be accessed online from home. One such service is checking and downloading your **Pollution Under Control (PUC) Certificate**.

A **PUC certificate** is a government-issued document that confirms your vehicle’s emissions are within the permissible pollution limits set by authorities. It is a **mandatory document** that every vehicle must have while driving on Indian roads.

You can check or download your certificate through the official Parivahan portal:
https://puc.parivahan.gov.in/puc/

---

# What is a PUC Certificate?

A **Pollution Under Control (PUC) certificate** confirms that your vehicle’s emissions are within the allowed limits defined by environmental regulations.

It is issued after your vehicle undergoes an **emission test at an authorised PUC centre**.

Under the **Motor Vehicles Act, 1988**, driving a vehicle without a valid PUC certificate can result in penalties.

### Why the PUC Test is Important

* Ensures vehicles emit pollutants within permissible limits
* Helps reduce environmental pollution
* Detects potential engine issues early
* Allows vehicles to legally operate on public roads
* Required while renewing vehicle insurance

---

# Components of a PUC Certificate

### PUC Serial Number

Each certificate has a **unique serial number** used for tracking and verification.

### Vehicle Details

Includes vehicle information such as:

* Vehicle registration number
* Make and model
* Fuel type

### Issue Date and Expiry Date

The certificate displays the **date of issue and expiry**.

Validity:

* **New vehicles:** 1 year
* **Older vehicles:** 6 months

### Emission Readings

The certificate includes emission readings such as:

* **CO (Carbon Monoxide)**
* **HC (Hydrocarbons)**

These values confirm whether the vehicle complies with emission standards.

# How to Download a PUC Certificate Online

You can download or print your PUC certificate online through the **Parivahan portal**.

**Step 1:** Visit the official PUC portal:
https://puc.parivahan.gov.in/puc/

**Step 2:** Click on the **“PUC Certificate”** option on the homepage.

**Step 3:** Enter the required details:

* Vehicle registration number
* Last **5 digits of the chassis number**

**Step 4:** Enter the **captcha code**.

**Step 5:** Click **“PUC Details”** to view your certificate.

If your vehicle has a valid PUC certificate, it will be displayed on the screen and you can **download or print it for your records**.

Note: You can **only download an existing valid certificate online**. To obtain a new certificate or renew an expired one, you must visit an authorised PUC centre.

---

# How to Find the Nearest PUC Centre

You can find authorised emission testing centres using the Parivahan portal.

**Step 1:** Visit the PUC portal:
https://puc.parivahan.gov.in/puc/

**Step 2:** Click **“PUC Center List.”**

**Step 3:** Select your **state and district**.

**Step 4:** The portal will display a list of **RTO-authorised PUC centres**, including:

* Address
* Contact details
* Vehicle testing availability

Always obtain your certificate from **authorised centres only**.

---

# How to Check PUC Application Status Online

If you applied for a new PUC certificate, you can check its status online.

**Step 1:** Visit the Parivahan portal:
https://parivahan.gov.in/parivahan/

**Step 2:** Click **“Online Services.”**

**Step 3:** Select **“Application Status.”**

**Step 4:** Choose the application type:

* Registration
* Renewal

**Step 5:** Enter your **application ID**.

**Step 6:** Enter the captcha and click **Submit** to view the status.

---

# How to Check PUC Certificate Validity

You can check whether your certificate is valid by following these steps.

**Step 1:** Visit the PUC portal:
https://puc.parivahan.gov.in/puc/

**Step 2:** Click **“PUC Certificate.”**

**Step 3:** Enter:

* Vehicle registration number
* Last 5 digits of the chassis number
* Captcha code

**Step 4:** Submit the form to view the validity details of your certificate.

# Is PUC Mandatory for Vehicle Insurance?

Yes. The **Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI)** requires vehicle owners to have a **valid PUC certificate** while renewing motor insurance.

This rule was introduced in collaboration with the **Environment Pollution (Prevention and Control) Authority (EPCA)** to control vehicle emissions.

Without a valid PUC certificate, insurance renewal may not be processed.

---

# Conclusion

A valid **PUC certificate** ensures that your vehicle meets environmental emission standards and complies with legal requirements. It also helps maintain your vehicle’s engine health and allows you to drive legally on public roads.

With the **Parivahan portal**, vehicle owners can easily check their certificate validity, download their PUC certificate, and locate authorised testing centres online.

Always ensure your certificate is **renewed before expiry** to avoid penalties and maintain compliance with vehicle regulations.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### Do I need a new PUC certificate when travelling to another state?

No. A valid PUC certificate issued in one state is **valid across all states in India**.

---

### What documents must I carry while driving?

You must carry the following documents:

* Driving Licence
* Vehicle Registration Certificate (RC)
* Valid Vehicle Insurance
* Pollution Under Control (PUC) Certificate

---

### Can I renew my PUC certificate before it expires?

Yes. It is advisable to renew your certificate **before the expiry date** to avoid penalties or legal issues.
