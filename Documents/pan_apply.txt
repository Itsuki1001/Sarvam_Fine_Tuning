# How to Apply for PAN Card Online and Offline in India

A **Permanent Account Number (PAN)** is a unique identification number issued by the **Income Tax Department of India**. It is required for filing income tax returns, opening bank accounts, investing in financial instruments, and conducting high-value financial transactions.

You can apply for a PAN card **online or offline** through authorised government portals.

---

# Key Highlights

* PAN card can be applied online through the **Income Tax Portal, NSDL (Protean), or UTIITSL**.
* **Instant e-PAN** is available for individuals with an **Aadhaar-linked mobile number**.
* PAN application fees vary depending on **dispatch mode and applicant location**.
* PAN card corrections or updates can also be requested online.
* Companies and LLPs receive **PAN automatically during company incorporation**.

---

# How to Apply for PAN Card Online

PAN card applications can be submitted online through the following authorised portals:

1. Income Tax e-Filing Portal (Instant e-PAN)
2. NSDL (Protean eGov Technologies) Portal
3. UTIITSL Portal

Online applications are usually **faster and allow easy tracking of application status**.

---

# Method 1: Apply for Instant e-PAN via Income Tax Portal

Individuals with a **valid Aadhaar number and Aadhaar-linked mobile number** can apply for an **Instant e-PAN**.

Official Portal:
https://www.incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal/

**Step 1:** Visit the Income Tax e-Filing portal:
https://www.incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal/

**Step 2:** Click **“Instant e-PAN”**.

**Step 3:** Select **“Get New e-PAN.”**

**Step 4:** Enter your **Aadhaar number** and click **Continue**.

**Step 5:** Accept the declaration and enter the **OTP received on your Aadhaar-linked mobile number**.

**Step 6:** Verify the details fetched from Aadhaar and confirm.

After successful verification, an **acknowledgement number** will be generated.
The **e-PAN is usually issued within a few minutes** and can be downloaded from the portal.

Note:

* Instant e-PAN is available **only for individual Indian citizens**.
* Applicants must be **above 18 years of age** with an Aadhaar-linked mobile number.

---

# Method 2: Apply for PAN Card Online via NSDL (Protean)

Applicants can also apply for a PAN card using **Form 49A (Indian citizens)** or **Form 49AA (foreign citizens)**.

Official Portal:
https://www.onlineservices.nsdl.com/paam/endUserRegisterContact.html

**Step 1:** Visit the NSDL PAN application website.

**Step 2:** Select the application type:

* **New PAN – Indian Citizen (Form 49A)**
* **New PAN – Foreign Citizen (Form 49AA)**

**Step 3:** Enter basic details such as:

* Name
* Date of birth
* Email ID
* Mobile number

**Step 4:** Submit the form to generate a **Token Number**.

**Step 5:** Continue filling the PAN application form by providing:

* Personal details
* Contact details
* AO code
* Document details

**Step 6:** Upload the required documents.

**Step 7:** Pay the PAN application fee through available payment options.

After successful submission, you will receive an **acknowledgement number** to track the application.

Once verified, the **PAN card will be issued and delivered within approximately 15–20 days**.

---

# Method 3: Apply for PAN Card Online via UTIITSL

Applicants can also apply through the **UTI Infrastructure Technology and Services Limited (UTIITSL)** portal.

Official Portal:
https://www.pan.utiitsl.com/

**Step 1:** Visit the UTIITSL PAN portal.

**Step 2:** Click **“Apply for New PAN Card.”**

**Step 3:** Select the appropriate form:

* **Form 49A** for Indian citizens
* **Form 49AA** for foreign citizens

**Step 4:** Fill in the required details such as:

* Personal information
* Address details
* Parent details
* Document details

**Step 5:** Upload the required documents.

**Step 6:** Pay the application fee.

After submission, an **acknowledgement number will be generated**.
The PAN card is usually **issued within 15 days after verification**.

---

# How to Apply for PAN Card Offline

Applicants can also apply for PAN through offline mode.

**Step 1:** Obtain **Form 49A or Form 49AA** from a PAN service centre or download it online.

**Step 2:** Fill out the application form carefully.

**Step 3:** Attach:

* Passport-size photographs
* Signature
* Required supporting documents

**Step 4:** Submit the form at the nearest **PAN service centre**.

**Step 5:** Pay the PAN application fee.

You will receive an **acknowledgement number** which can be used to track the application status.

After verification, the PAN card is usually issued within **15–20 days**.

---

# Eligibility for PAN Card

The following individuals and entities can apply for PAN:

* Indian citizens aged **18 years and above**
* **Minors** (through guardians)
* **Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs)**
* **Companies and LLPs**
* **Partnership firms**
* **Trusts and associations**
* **Foreign nationals and NRIs**

---

# Documents Required for PAN Card

Applicants must submit supporting documents along with the application.

### Identity Proof

Examples include:

* Aadhaar card
* Passport
* Voter ID
* Driving licence

### Address Proof

Examples include:

* Aadhaar card
* Utility bills
* Bank statements
* Passport

### Date of Birth Proof

Examples include:

* Birth certificate
* Aadhaar card
* Passport
* School leaving certificate

Companies and other entities must submit **registration certificates or incorporation documents**.

# PAN Card Application Fees

| Mode of Application | Dispatch Mode              | Fee (Approx.) |
| ------------------- | -------------------------- | ------------- |
| Online/Offline      | Physical PAN within India  | ₹107          |
| Online/Offline      | Physical PAN outside India | ₹1,017        |
| Online (Paperless)  | Physical PAN within India  | ₹101          |
| Online/Offline      | e-PAN sent via email       | ₹66 – ₹72     |

---

# Automatic PAN Allotment for Companies

For companies and LLPs, **PAN is automatically allotted during company incorporation** through the **Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA)** system.

No separate PAN application is required.

---

# How to Update or Correct PAN Card Details

If you need to update details such as **name, date of birth, or address**, you can request a correction online.

**Step 1:** Visit either the NSDL or UTIITSL portal.

**Step 2:** Select **“Changes or Corrections in Existing PAN / Reprint of PAN Card.”**

**Step 3:** Enter the details to be updated and upload supporting documents.

**Step 4:** Pay the correction processing fee.

After verification, the **updated PAN card will be issued within approximately 15 days**.

---

# Summary

A PAN card is an essential identity and financial document required for many activities such as filing income tax returns, opening bank accounts, and making large financial transactions.

Applicants can easily apply for PAN through the **Income Tax portal, NSDL, or UTIITSL**, either online or offline. With options like **Instant e-PAN**, the process has become faster and more convenient for individuals.
