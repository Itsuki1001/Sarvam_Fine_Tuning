# RC Transfer: How to Transfer Vehicle Ownership Online & Offline

RC transfer, or vehicle ownership transfer, is the process of shifting the legal ownership of a vehicle from one person to another. This usually happens when a vehicle is **sold, inherited, or purchased through an auction**.

Vehicle ownership transfer can be done through the **RTO (Regional Transport Office)** or online through the official Parivahan portal.

Official Portal:
https://parivahan.gov.in/parivahan/

Completing the RC transfer ensures that the **new owner becomes the legal owner of the vehicle** and prevents the previous owner from facing legal liabilities related to the vehicle.

---

# Key Highlights

* **RC transfer is required** when selling, inheriting, or purchasing a vehicle so the new owner is officially registered.
* **Important documents** such as **Form 29, Form 30, and the original RC** are required for a smooth ownership transfer.
* **NOC from RTO** is required if the vehicle ownership is transferred to another state.

---

# When to Transfer Vehicle Ownership

RC transfer is required in the following situations:

### When Selling or Buying a Vehicle

Ownership must be transferred when a vehicle is sold so that the buyer becomes the legally recognised owner.

### After Inheritance

If a vehicle is inherited after the owner's death, the ownership must be transferred to the legal heir.

### After Public Auction Purchase

If a vehicle is purchased through a public auction, ownership must be transferred so the buyer is officially registered as the new owner.

---

# Why RC Transfer Is Important

### Legal Protection

It ensures the previous owner is not held responsible for accidents, violations, or liabilities involving the vehicle.

### Clear Ownership

RC transfer establishes clear ownership and prevents disputes.

### Access to Vehicle Services

It allows the new owner to renew insurance, pay road tax, or access vehicle services easily.

---

# Essential Documents for Vehicle Ownership Transfer

The following documents are commonly required for RC transfer:

* Original RC (Registration Certificate)
* Insurance certificate
* Pollution Under Control (PUC) certificate
* PAN card or Form 60
* Chassis and engine pencil print
* Address proof of the new owner
* Passport-size photographs of buyer and seller

---

# Additional Documents Based on Transfer Type

### For Normal Vehicle Sale

* Form 29
* Form 30
* Proof of date of birth of buyer
* Purchaser’s undertaking
* Tax clearance certificate

### For Ownership Transfer After Owner’s Death

* Form 31
* Death certificate
* Proof of legal succession

### For Public Auction Purchase

* Form 32
* Sale confirmation order from auction authority
* Certified auction approval

# How to Transfer Vehicle Ownership Online

Vehicle ownership transfer can be initiated through the **Parivahan portal**.

**Step 1:** Visit the official Parivahan portal:
https://parivahan.gov.in/parivahan/

**Step 2:** Click **“Online Services”** and select **“Vehicle Related Services.”**

**Step 3:** Enter your **vehicle registration number** and click **Proceed**.

**Step 4:** Select **“Miscellaneous (TO/CoA/HPA/HPS/HPT/DupRC)”**.

**Step 5:** Enter your **mobile number** and generate an **OTP**.

**Step 6:** After OTP verification, select **“Transfer of Ownership.”**

**Step 7:** Fill in the required details such as:

* New owner information
* Address details
* Insurance details

**Step 8:** Pay the applicable fee online.

**Step 9:** After payment, the system generates **receipts and Forms 29 & 30**.

**Step 10:** Both buyer and seller must **sign the forms** and submit them along with:

* Original RC
* Insurance certificate
* PUC certificate

These documents must be submitted to the **local RTO** for verification.

**Step 11:** After successful verification, the **new RC will be issued in the buyer’s name**. The updated RC is usually sent by post.

# Common Mistakes to Avoid During RC Transfer

### Incomplete Documents

Missing documents can delay the process or lead to rejection.

### Incorrect Information

Ensure all forms are filled accurately and match the official documents.

### Delay in Transfer

Ownership transfer should be completed quickly after selling the vehicle to avoid legal risks.

### Not Checking Fees

Be aware of the applicable RTO charges before initiating the process.

### Ignoring Insurance Transfer

Motor insurance must also be transferred to the new owner to maintain legal coverage.

---

# Summary

RC transfer is an important step whenever a vehicle changes ownership. Completing the transfer ensures legal protection for both the buyer and seller and allows the new owner to access vehicle services without issues. By following the correct process and submitting the required documents, you can complete the vehicle ownership transfer smoothly.

# FAQs

### Q1. When should you apply for RC transfer after selling the vehicle?

You should apply for RC transfer **within 14 days of the sale** to avoid penalties and legal complications.

---

### Q2. What is the RC transfer process for renting a vehicle?

RC transfer is **not required for renting a vehicle**. The owner only needs to submit a rental agreement to the RTO.

---

### Q3. What happens if I don’t transfer vehicle ownership after buying a used car?

If ownership is not transferred, the **previous owner will still be legally responsible** for accidents, traffic violations, or other liabilities involving the vehicle.

---

### Q4. Can I transfer vehicle ownership if the vehicle has a hypothecation?

Yes, but you must obtain a **No Objection Certificate (NOC)** from the financier or bank before proceeding with the transfer.

---

### Q5. Is a valid insurance policy required for RC transfer?

Yes, a **valid vehicle insurance policy is mandatory** during the RC transfer process.
