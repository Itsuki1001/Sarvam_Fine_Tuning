# RC Renewal After 15 Years: How to Renew Vehicle Registration Online & Offline

Under the **Motor Vehicles Act, 1988**, every vehicle must have a valid **Registration Certificate (RC)** to legally operate on public roads in India. The RC is issued by the **Regional Transport Office (RTO)** and confirms that your vehicle is officially registered with the government.

A vehicle's registration certificate is valid for **15 years from the date of issue**. After this period, the vehicle owner must **renew the RC** to continue using the vehicle legally.

Ideally, the renewal process should be started **at least 60 days before the expiry of the 15-year validity period**. Once renewed, the registration is usually extended for **5 years**, subject to the vehicle passing a **fitness inspection**.

Driving with an expired RC can result in **penalties and legal action**.

---

# Preparing for Car Registration Renewal

Before applying for RC renewal, keep the following documents ready:

* Duly filled **Form 25** (Application for renewal of RC)
* Original **Registration Certificate (RC)**
* **Pollution Under Control (PUC) certificate**
* **Fitness certificate**
* Proof of **road tax payment** (if applicable)
* **Car insurance policy certificate**
* Copy of **PAN card** or **Form 60 / Form 61**
* **Chassis and engine pencil print**
* Owner’s **signature identification**

Note: The exact document requirements may vary slightly between states.

---

# How to Renew Car Registration After 15 Years (Online)

Vehicle registration renewal can be initiated through the official **Parivahan portal**.

Official Portal:
https://parivahan.gov.in/parivahan/

**Step 1:** Visit the Parivahan website:
https://parivahan.gov.in/parivahan/

**Step 2:** Click **“Online Services”** and select **“Vehicle Related Services.”**

**Step 3:** Select your **state of residence**.

**Step 4:** Choose your **nearest RTO** and click **“Proceed.”**

**Step 5:** Select **“RC Related Services.”**

**Step 6:** Choose **“Renewal of Registration.”**

**Step 7:** Enter the required vehicle details such as **registration number**.

**Step 8:** Click **“Verify Details.”**

**Step 9:** Pay any **pending road tax or renewal fees online**.

**Step 10:** After payment, **schedule an appointment at the RTO** for vehicle inspection.

Once the vehicle passes inspection, the **renewed RC will be issued**.

---

# Offline Process for RC Renewal After 15 Years

Vehicle registration can also be renewed by visiting the RTO directly.

**Step 1:** Fill out **Form 25**.

**Step 2:** Submit the form along with the required documents at the RTO where the vehicle was originally registered.

**Step 3:** Pay the applicable **renewal fees and taxes** at the RTO counter.

**Step 4:** The vehicle will undergo a **fitness inspection** by the registering authority.

**Step 5:** Pay the **re-registration fee** and collect the payment receipt.

**Step 6:** After approval, the **renewed RC will be sent by post or issued by the RTO**.

---

# Re-Registration of Vehicle for Inter-State Transfer

If you permanently move to another state, the vehicle must be re-registered in that state.

The process involves the following steps:

1. Obtain a **No Objection Certificate (NOC)** from the original RTO.
2. Transport the vehicle to the new state.
3. Submit the **NOC**, valid **insurance**, and **PUC certificate** to the new RTO.
4. Fill and submit:

   * **Form 20** (Application for registration)
   * **Form 27** (Application for new registration mark)
5. Pay the applicable **road tax** in the new state.
6. The vehicle will undergo **inspection and chassis verification**.

After verification, the **new Registration Certificate will be issued** with the updated state registration.

Failure to complete the transfer within **12 months of relocation** may result in penalties.

# How to Check RC Renewal Status Online

You can check the status of your RC renewal application online.

**Step 1:** Visit the Parivahan portal:
https://parivahan.gov.in/parivahan/

**Step 2:** Click **“Online Services”** and select **“Vehicle Related Services.”**

**Step 3:** Select your **state**.

**Step 4:** Choose the RTO where you applied and click **“Proceed.”**

**Step 5:** Select **“Know Your Application Status.”**

**Step 6:** Enter your **application number or vehicle registration number**.

**Step 7:** Click **“Submit”** to view the RC renewal status.

# Importance of Car Insurance

Car insurance is an essential protection for your vehicle and is **mandatory under Indian law**.

### Financial Protection

Insurance covers repair costs or financial liabilities in case of accidents or damage.

### Legal Requirement

At least **third-party motor insurance** is mandatory under the **Motor Vehicles Act, 1988**.

### Protection Against Natural Disasters

Comprehensive insurance can cover damage caused by floods, earthquakes, cyclones, and other natural disasters.

### Personal Accident Cover

Many policies offer personal accident coverage for the **owner-driver**.

# Summary

A **Registration Certificate (RC)** proves that your vehicle is officially registered with the government. Since the validity of an RC is **15 years**, renewing it on time is essential to continue using your vehicle legally.

By using the **Parivahan portal**, vehicle owners can apply for RC renewal, check application status, and manage other vehicle-related services conveniently online.

# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### What is the cost of renewing car registration after 15 years?

The total cost typically ranges between **₹5,000 and ₹15,000**, depending on factors such as:

* State rules
* Fitness certificate charges
* Green tax
* Late fees (if applicable)

The base RC renewal fee itself is usually much lower.

---

### What can I do if my car is more than 15 years old?

You can apply for **re-registration along with a fitness certificate**. However, some regions such as **Delhi-NCR have restrictions on older vehicles**.

---

### Can a 20-year-old car be registered in India?

Yes, provided the vehicle **passes the fitness test and meets emission norms**. However, restrictions apply in some regions.

---

### Can we drive a car after 15 years?

Yes. You can drive the car after 15 years **if the RC is renewed and the vehicle passes the fitness test**. In areas with strict pollution laws, older vehicles may still be restricted.
